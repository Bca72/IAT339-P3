Daisy 7094

Project Overview

Daisy 7094 is an interactive art installation that turns the participants’ conversations into geometric artworks that can then be played back in real time.

Daisy 7094 was created for my Interactive Arts class in the winter of 2022.

Ideation Process

When we were first assigned the task to create an interactive art piece, I wanted participants to be able to walk away from our installation with a customized piece of artwork. After a few more ideation sessions with my group, I came up with the initial idea for Daisy 7094, which was to record a conversation between two random participants and then paint on a canvas using the sound data that we grabbed from microphones.

Design Process

My original idea for the final output was to create an image that was generated based on the voice data of the participants. However, this version of the output ended up looking too generic and the uniqueness of each participant was not being captured. After getting some advice from my professor I realized that the participants should be able to “read” the final output to an extent. If they did not know what the final image represented, then the artwork would lose meaning.

Challenges

The biggest challenge I faced while working on this project was trying to let the participants distinguish themselves. At first, I tried only using colour to distinguish between participants but that felt a bit too arbitrary. So, I thought about the vocal aspects that distinguished people in a conversation. The main aspects I came up with are their tone of voice while speaking, how loud they were speaking and when they chose not to speak.

With these three aspects as well as letting the participants choose a color that they felt represented them, I could create a connection between the participants and the artwork. When the participants watched the final output, they would be able to recall some aspects of the conversation based on just the visual representation.

Reflection

Daisy 7094 had a very positive reception during the day of the showcase. So much so that we also presented at the 2023 FCAT Undergraduate Conference. Through working on this project, I was able to practice my skills in ideation and design. Most importantly, I was able to develop my cooperation and communication skills.
